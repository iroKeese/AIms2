# Literature Organization

## Theme 1: Algorithm Aversion vs. Appreciation

### Core Papers
- **Dietvorst, B. J., Simmons, J. P., & Massey, C. (2015)**. Algorithm aversion: People erroneously avoid algorithms after seeing them err. *Journal of Experimental Psychology: General*, 144(1), 114.
  - Key finding: People avoid algorithms after observing errors
  - Relevance: Establishes algorithm aversion phenomenon

- **Logg, J. M., Minson, J. A., & Moore, D. A. (2019)**. Algorithm appreciation: People prefer algorithmic to human judgment. *Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes*, 151, 90-103.
  - Key finding: People prefer algorithmic judgment over human judgment
  - Relevance: Establishes algorithm appreciation phenomenon

### Reconciling the Two
- **Castelo, N., Bos, M. W., & Lehmann, D. R. (2019)**. Task-dependent algorithm aversion. *Journal of Marketing Research*, 56(5), 809-825.
  - Key finding: Algorithm aversion/appreciation depends on task type (objective vs. subjective)
  - Relevance: Explains when people prefer algorithms

- **Schaap, G., Bosse, T., & Hendriks Vettehen, P. (2023)**. The ABC of algorithmic aversion: Not agent, but benefits and control determine the acceptance of automated decision-making. *AI & Society*, 1-14.
  - Key finding: Benefits and control, not agent type, determine acceptance
  - Relevance: Factors influencing algorithm acceptance

- **Morewedge, C. K. (2022)**. Preference for human, not algorithm aversion. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 26(10), 824-826.
  - Key finding: Algorithm aversion originates from biased human self-evaluations
  - Relevance: Theoretical framework for understanding algorithm aversion

### Domain-Specific Studies
- **Longoni, C., Bonezzi, A., & Morewedge, C. K. (2019)**. Resistance to medical artificial intelligence. *Journal of Consumer Research*, 46(4), 629-650.
  - Key finding: People resist AI in medical decisions due to uniqueness concerns
  - Relevance: Domain-specific algorithm aversion

- **Jussupow, E., Benbasat, I., & Heinzl, A. (2020)**. Why are we averse towards algorithms? A comprehensive literature review on algorithm aversion.
  - Key finding: Review of algorithm aversion literature
  - Relevance: Comprehensive overview

## Theme 2: Trust and Reliance in AI

## Notes for Writing

- **Balance**: Aim for 30-40 references total
- **Recency**: Include recent papers (2024-2025) where possible
- **Relevance**: Only cite papers directly relevant to your study
- **Integration**: Weave references into narrative, don't just list them
- **Critical evaluation**: Don't just describe, evaluate and connect
- **Positioning**: Clearly show how your study contributes
- **CHB style**: Follow journal's citation format
