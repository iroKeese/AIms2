## Stage: InstructionsPart2b


Part 2 - Instructions (1/3)
Please make sure you read the instructions carefully. You again will have to answer several comprehension questions correctly afterwards in order to continue with the study.
In Part 2 of the study, you will complete 8 tasks . In each task, you will be presented with a table of options again, much like in Part 1. However, in Part 2, each table consists of 8 options . Recall that in this part, Points you earn will be converted to money payment using an exchange rate of 50 Points = Ł1.00.
In principle, the logic of the table and the way how payoffs are determined are exactly the same as in Part 1: each option has 10 outcomes, and a roll of a 10-sided die will determine the actual payoff your chosen option will yield. The main difference to Part 1 is that in Part 2, you can either choose an option yourself, or delegate the decision to a computer. In addition, if you decide to choose the option yourself, you will be facing a time limit.

## Stage: InstructionsPart2c


Part 2 - Instructions (2/3)
Details. Each task proceeds as follows:
1. You are shown the table of options for < | 1> seconds.
2. You have < | 1> seconds to decide on how your option should be selected:
(a) you select the lottery yourself
(b) you have a computer select the lottery for you
3. The table of options is shown for another < | 1> seconds. If you picked (a), you need to choose an option during these < | 1> seconds.
If you decide to have the computer select a lottery for you, its choice will be determined by an algorithm. This algorithm works out how much each option would yield on average, and selects the option for which this is highest.

## Stage: InstructionsPart2d


Part 2 - Instructions (3/3)
Note that having the computer select the option for you does not come with any additional costs. You will make the decision on whether it is you or the computer who selects the option for each task anew, and your decision in one task will not affect any other task in any way.
After completing a task, regardless of whether you decided to select the option yourself or have the computer select it for you, you will be shown the selected option, the outcome of the die roll, and the Points you will earn if this task is randomly selected for payment.
Lastly, you will be asked after every task to rate your satisfaction with the lottery selection for that task on a scale of 1 to 5 diamonds, where 1 diamond means "not satisfied at all" and 5 diamonds means "completely satisfied".
